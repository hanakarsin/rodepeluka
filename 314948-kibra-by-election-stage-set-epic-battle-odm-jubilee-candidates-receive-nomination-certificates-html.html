Kibra by-election: Stage set for epic battle as ODM, Jubilee candidates receive nomination certifica

- The Orange party also officially launched campaign for their candidate and dared other parties to get ready

- The party's leader Raila announced Junet Mohamed, ODM party's director of elections, will lead the campaigns

- Other candidates in the race include Jubilee Party's Mariga, Amani National Congress party’s Owalo and Butichi from Ford Kenya

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party has officially unveiled its flag bearer, Bernard Otieno Okoth alias Imran, in readiness for Kibra by-election slated for November 7, 2019.

The Orange party on Tuesday, September 10, handed nomination certificate to Imran who floored nine other aspirants in the primaries to clinch the party's ticket, and declared they were now ready to hit the ground running.

In a ceremony graced by its leader Raila Odinga, ODM put on notice all competitors in the race saying they had assembled a campaign team and are confident the victory will be theirs.

"Our party conducted primaries and all the candidates came together after the results and agreed to support whoever won. The winner was none other than Benard Imran Otieno Okoth. The other candidates have agreed they will support him," Raila said.

The former prime minister also announced Junet Mohamed, ODM party's director of elections, will lead the party's by-election campaigns.

"The only party that has conducted nominations for the Kibra by-election is ODM, the rest did not do any nomination. I want to tell Jubilee, ANC, Tanga Tanga and others...get ready," Mohamed declared.

The party's main man, Imran, will face off with candidates from other parties, including Jubilee Party's Mcdonald Mariga, a footballer turned politician, Amani National Congress (ANC) party’s Eliud Owalo and Khamisi Butichi from Ford Kenya.

Mariga, just like Imran, was also expected to receive his nomination certificate on Tuesday and both were to formally presents their candidatures to the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC).

The Kibra parliamentary seat fell vacant following the death of the area MP Ken Okoth on the afternoon of Friday, July 26, 2019.

Okoth was battling colorectal cancer and was on treatment for nearly seven months both locally and abroad.

Homa Bay: Fear Grips Fishermen after Discovering 5 Explosives In Lake Victoria

  • The fishermen were in their routine expedition when they came across the explosives
  • Their vessel's anchor was attached to a metallic box that had been housing the devices
  • They immediately informed the police who took charge of the situation, later contacting the bomb experts expected to destroy the explosives

Five bombs were discovered by fishermen on Thursday, September 16, in the waters of Lake Victoria at Litare beach in Rusinga Island within Homa Bay county. 

Litare Beach Management Unit chairperson Isiah Ochieng explained that the explosive devices were discovered by a group of fishermen who had been on their daily fishing expedition.

He elucidated that the fishermen had dropped their anchor to support their boat only to realize it was abnormally heavy when they tried to retrieve it making them excited about the prospect of a huge catch.

“The bombs were discovered by a group of fishermen who went fishing for Omena near Ngodhe Island when they pulled a heavy metallic box containing the explosives, which they had caught using their boat anchor,” explained Ochieng.

Ochieng added that he was immediately called by the fishermen after which he informed police officers from Mbita police post. 

The chairperson, however, expressed concern on the frequency with which fishermen in the region discovered explosive devices saying this was the third time in a span of three months that such an incident had occurred on the same beach. 

Mbita Sub- County Police Commander Stanley Atavachi, who also confirmed the incident, said that the bombs are currently in safe custody and will be dismantled by experts.

This comes barely a month after six mortar bombs were discovered by fishermen in the same county.

Fishermen discover mortar bombs

As narrated by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), the fishermen had netted a heavy metallic box thinking it was fish.

In a bid to discover what was contained in the box, they hastily broke into it and found six, 60mm mortar bombs, staring at them!

The shocked fishermen could not hide their fear, some of them opting to jump into the waters for the fear that the bombs would go off.

They steered the boat ashore and reported the incident to the beach management chairman, Isaiah Ouko, who then escalated the matter to DCI detectives based at Mbita.

The Mbita based sleuths immediately notified their Bomb Disposal and Hazardous Materials counterparts based in Kisumu, who rushed to Mbita and took possession of the cache.

Upon conducting their investigations, the Kisumu-based detectives destroyed the bombs in accordance with the law established.

Uhuru honours barman with Head of State Commendation

- President Uhuru Kenyatta has rewarded retired parliament barman Andrew Gicheha with Head of State Commendation for his 33-year dedicated service to the lawmakers

- Gicheha said Uhuru, who was once his Gatundu MP, was one of his favourite patrons alongside former president Mwai Kibaki and the late John Michuki 

President Uhuru Kenyatta has awarded a barman who used to serve him when he was a member of parliament.

Andrew Gicheha was, on Tuesday, April 19, delighted to receive Head of State Commendation for his outstanding work at the parliament pub.

The 57-year-old retired in 2014 after serving in parliament for 33 years. He started as a waiter and first as a waiter in the restaurant and later became a barman.

The award was presented to him by Senate Speaker Ekwee Ethuro.

He said some of his favourite patrons were Uhuru, former president Mwai Kibaki and the late John Michuki.

The state commendations are usually awarded by the president upon recommendation by the National Honours and Awards Committee.

Other state awards and honours in Kenya are: Order of the Golden Heart of Kenya, Order of the Burning Spear, Order of the Grand Warrior of Kenya, Distinguished Conduct Order, Distinguished Service Medal and Silver Star of Kenya.

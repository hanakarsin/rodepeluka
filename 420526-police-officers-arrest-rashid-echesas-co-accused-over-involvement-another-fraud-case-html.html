There was a moment of drama at the Milimani Law Courts after former sports Cabinet secretary Rashid Echesa's co-accused Chrispine Oduor Odipo got arrested in court for defrauding a business lady KSh 810,000 by false pretences.

Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) pounced on him as he left the courtroom where he had attended the KSh 39 billion fake arms trial.

Oduor who a director of Pzel Company Limited allegedly received KSh 11.5 million kickback on behalf of Echesa and others in the Sh 40 billion fake arms scandal.

In the current matter where he was arrested on the morning of Thursday, July 29, Odour was accused of obtaining KSh 810,000 from Joyce Bosibori pretending he was in a position to supply lift repair materials.

Oduor is also a wanted suspect by Kilimani detectives over another case of fraud involving KSh 14 million.

The sleuths from DCl are said to have driven Odour to Ongata Rongai Police Station for further interrogation before he is charged with obtaining money by false pretences.

The officers were camping at the Milimani Law Courts for hours awaiting for Odour to conclude the mention of his KSh 39 billion case before senior principal magistrate Kenneth Cheruiyot.

Immediately the matter was taken out of the hearing by the magistrate, the officers pounced on Odour and handcuffed him and took him to the court cells before driving him away.

Additionally, the KSh 39 billion case has been adjourned until August 13, 2021, since the prosecution was not ready to proceed with the case.

Fake arms scandal

The fake arms scandal sent shockwaves across the country after Deputy President William Ruto's Harambee Annex office was adversely mentioned in the scam.

The contracted foreign investors said they met Echesa in DP's office and the former CS had promised them a meeting with the second in command to finalise the deal.

During the investigations, detectives raided Ruto's office where they went through CCTV footage for crucial evidence in connection to the fraudulent scheme.

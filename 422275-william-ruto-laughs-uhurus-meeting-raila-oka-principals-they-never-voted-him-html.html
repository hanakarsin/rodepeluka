William Ruto Laughs at Uhuru's Meeting with Raila, OKA Principals: &quot;They Never Voted for Him&quot;

  • Deputy President William Ruto aired his sentiments over the recent Mombasa meeting between Uhuru, Raila and OKA big wigs
  • In a dismissive fashion, the second in command said it was puzzling for the politicians to meet a president whom they have never voted for or supported
  • Ruto said Raila and his counterparts were driven by selfish agendas in meeting the head of state on Tuesday, August 10

Deputy President William Ruto has mocked the recent meeting between President Uhuru Kenyatta, Raila Odinga and One Kenya Alliance (OKA) principals.

Uhuru met Raila, his deputy Wycliffe Oparanya, Wiper's Kalonzo Musyoka, Amani National Congress boss Musalia Mudavadi, Ford Kenya's Moses Wetang'ula and KANU party leader Gideon Moi at State House Mombasa on Tuesday, August 10.

Speaking after meeting grassroots leaders from Ruiru at his Karen residence on Thursday, August 12, Ruto said Raila and his counterparts have never voted nor supported the head of state and were only holding meetings with him to push their own selfish interests.

"Those six men have never voted for Uhuru Kenyatta. They have never even supported him to fulfil the pledges he made to Kenyans. They went to meet him over their own selfish agenda," he said.

The deputy president noted the thirst for power and government positions was the motivation behind the meeting between the political big wigs which sparked political debates in various forums.

"They want power and positions. They want to use the office which we helped the president to get so that they can fight with us. Shouldn't we just leave these people to God," he posed to his audience.

Looming Cabinet reshuffle

Besides succession politics, Uhuru is reported to have disclosed to the Opposition leaders that a Cabinet reshuffle was in the works during the meeting.

The first command hinted at appointing some of the leaders' loyalists into plum government positions.

Raila-Kalonzo cold war

At the same time, the bad blood between Raila and Kalonzo played out during the Mombasa meeting after the duo failed to acknowledge each other while leaving the closed-door meeting.

The Orange Supremo seemed to be on good terms with everyone except Kalonzo, who also appeared unbothered by Raila's body language towards him.

The ODM chief greeted all the other politicians but overlooked Kalonzo, raising eyebrows among Kenyans.

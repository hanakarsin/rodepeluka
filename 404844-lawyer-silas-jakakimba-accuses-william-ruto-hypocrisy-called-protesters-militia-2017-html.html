Lawyer Silas Chepkeres Jakakimba has accused Deputy President William Ruto of hypocrisy hours after the conclusion of various by-elections across the country.

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) conducted mini-polls on two constituencies and four wards where instances of violence and chaos were witnessed.

Taking to his Twitter on Thursday, March 4, Jakakimba recalled the interview that the DP had with the BBC in 2017 where he called protestors militia.

"We ask DP to take a look at the video clip where he addressed a live interview on BBCWorld regarding what he termed 'militias'. We refuse hypocrisy," he said.

His sentiments came after supporters and politicians linked to the DP's United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party were caught on the wrong side of the law during the by-elections

Senator Samson Cherargei, Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa were some of Ruto's allies who were arrested during Thursday's mini polls.

Reports indicate the lawmakers were found with guns in their vehicles which were seen as a threat to voters.

Another Ruto point man, Rashid Echesa was captured on camera assaulting an IEBC official for failing to allow UDA agents to a polling station in Matungu constituency.

IEBC chairman Wafula Chebukati has since called for his arrest saying the incident was a direct attack on the Constitution.

Langata MP Nixon Korir, also a member of the UDA party, was attacked by charged youths and forced out of Milimani polling station over similar allegations.

Nyawa Murinzi: 5 Emotional Photos from Burial of Lamu Cop Who Died 2 Weeks after Wedding

  • Nyamu Murinzi was the Lamu sub-county police commander and he died after a short battle with COVID-19
  • Murinzi passed on just weeks after he married his beautiful bride in a grand ceremony in Kwale
  • He was laid to rest on Saturday, September 18, in an emotional send-off in Kwale

Lamu sub-county police boss Nyawa Murinzi who succumbed to COVID-19 has been laid to rest in an emotional ceremony in Kwale.

The 50-year-old senior police officer breathed his last on Sunday, September 12, after a weeks-long battle with COVID-19 which he contracted a few days after his lovely wedding.

The father of six wedded in a colourful ceremony on August 28, months after losing his first wife to cancer in October 2020.

According to the pictures shared, the deceased was laid to rest with full gun salutes and sobs from the police officers and mourners who gathered at the send-off.

Religious leaders from the coast region also attended the funeral to pay their last respects to the departed law enforcer who was also involved in church activities.

Speaking during the burial ceremony, the chairman of Coast Interfaith Council of Clerics Mohammed Abdulkadir the internment said Murinzi interacted well with everyone in Lamu.

As earlier reported, Murinzi's brother Peter Mwero Murinzi, said the father of six lost his wife in October 2020 to cancer and had just given his second stab at happiness on August 28, at a grand ceremony in Kwale.

He, unfortunately, fell ill days after his wedding and was initially diagnosed with malaria before being referred to the Diani Beach Hospital.

At the Diani Beach Hospital, he tested positive for COVID-19 and his deteriorating condition occasioned his transfer to the Coast General Hospital's Intensive Care Unit.

He, however, lost the battle on September 12, leaving his inconsolable new wife and six children.

Besides his work in the police force, the Lamu senior officer was also well-known for his work in the church.

Pastor Albert Hale said:

"It is a sad day for us and the GGO ministry after losing one of our frontline soldiers reverend Sammy Nyawa Murinzi. He was a great friend and a pillar of hope in the jungle where we build churches and minister to the vulnerable."

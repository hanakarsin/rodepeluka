Ugandan MP Bobi Wine outsmarts police, breaks out of house arrest

- Bobi was arrested while on his way to the venue of his Kyarenga concert

- Police drove him back to his house where they put him under house arrest

- Bobi cleverly sneaked out of his house without the knowledge of police and his whereabouts are still unknown

Kyadondo East Member of Parliament Bobi Wine on Wednesday, April 24, night pulled a fast on Ugandan police after slipping away from them while guarding him on his house arrest.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt the MP who was under house arrest outsmarted tens of police officers outside his house and disappeared into thin air.

According to a report by NTV Uganda, police launched a search for him after learning he had escaped but his whereabouts are yet to be established.

Bobi's escape came just hours after police arrested him in an unexpected move that was strongly condemned by millions of Ugandans.

As reported by a cross section of the East African media earlier, the MP was arrested on Monday, April 22, by police who accused him of failing to comply with safety precautions ahead of a Kyarenga concert set for the same day.

Armed police officers ambushed the MP as he tried to access the One Love Beach Busaabala which was the venue for his concert.

TUKO.co.ke understands police set up a roadblock, arrested the MP, bundled him into a guarded van and drove him back to his home where they kept watch on him.

Former KANU strongman and seasoned Nyayo era politician Hosea Mundui Kiplagat has passed on.

Kiplagat's family through spokesperson C.K Mundai confirmed the long serving KANU Baringo chairman died on Saturday, February 6, at The Karen Hospital in Nairobi where he had been admitted.

"The family of Hosea Mundui Kiplagat wishes to announce his passing on today, February 6, 2021 at 0930 hours, Karen Hospital, Nairobi," Mundui told media.

The businessman and former president Daniel arap Moi's maternal cousin was born at Kiplabal village in Baringo county in 1945.

Kiplaga was first employed as a prison warder but had to resign so as to try his hands in business where he became a successful business in Baringo.

His business influence captured Moi's attention who saw him as an efficient political mobiliser, a move that saw him become KANU Baringo branch chairperson.

As a grassroots mobiliser, Moi made him one of his political aides.

The 75-year-old politician tried his luck in mainstream politics in 2007 when he chose to run for Baringo Central parliamentary seat but lost to Sammy Mwaita.

He made launched another unsuccessful stab in 2013 in an election that saw Mwaita retain his seat prompting Kiplagat to petition the High Court which again upheld the win.

Kiplagat became the latest political figure to die after former presidential aspirant and MP Simeon Nyachae breathed his last five days ago.

The 88-year-old breathed his last at The Nairobi Hospital after a long battle with an undisclosed illness.

Martha Karua Asks Uhuru to Desist from Succession Politics, Finish His Term and Go

  • Martha Karua said President Uhuru Kenyatta should give 2022 politics a wide berth
  • Karua said the president cannot continue to hold Mt Kenya locals to ransom
  • The Narc Kenya leader said she met with Mwangi Kiunjuri and Moses Kuria to chart a clear path for the Mt Kenya region

Narc Kenya party leader Martha Karua has asked President Uhuru Kenyatta to desist from involving himself in succession politics and focus on finishing his term.

Karua said Kenyans, especially those from the Mt Kenya region have been supportive of the head of state ever since he took office but he should now give 2022 politics a wide berth and let the people decide the leaders they want.

Speaking during an interview with KTN, Karua said the president cannot continue to hold Mt Kenya locals on ransom adding that was the reason she met with other leaders from the mountain to consolidate the region ahead of the 2022 polls.

Additionally, she dismissed the Mombasa meeting between Uhuru and One Kenya Alliance leaders saying it was a gathering of big boys which they were allowed to hold.

As reported, Karua, Mwangi Kiunjuri (The Service Party) and Moses Kuria (Chama Cha Kazi) announced they were out to market the parties to other like-minded Kenyans.

Addressing the press at Serena Hotel in Nairobi on Thursday, August 12, Kiunjuri said they had started unifying one by one.

''As TSP, we are very clear; the way forward is that TSP will be on the ballot, the same way NARC-Kenya will be there and Chama Cha Kazi.

That one, there is no question about it. It is not whether we are hesitating to join any other political dispensation or we are adamant; the decision is made that TSP will be there in 2022,'' Kiunjuri said.

Mombasa meeting

President Uhuru Kenyatta on Tuesday, August 10, held a closed-door meeting with former NASA coalition principles.

According to a statement from State House, the leaders had a consultative meeting to discuss several subjects touching on the nation's state.

Political party leaders present were Raila Odinga (ODM), Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Kalonzo Musyoka (Wiper), Moses Wetangula (Ford Kenya), and Gideon Moi (KANU).

The meeting held at State House, Mombasa, was also attended by Kakamega governor and ODM deputy party leader Wycliffe Oparanya.

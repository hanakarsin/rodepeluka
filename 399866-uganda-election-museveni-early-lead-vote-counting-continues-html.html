Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni, 76, has taken an early lead against his closest challenger Robert Kyagulanyi, 38, alias Bobi Wine in the country's presidential election as vote counting continues.

According to provisional results by the country's Electoral Commission, the National Resistance Movement (NRM) leader has garnered 1.5 million votes as while Wine had gained 647,146 votes representing 27.39% from 23% of polling stations counted.

Wine's phone blocked

Wine had already hinted he would reject the results while dismissing the figures announced by the Commission.

The National Unity Platform (NUP) leader had on Thursday, January 14, smelled a rat after his phone was allegedly blocked by the government.

The politician's wife Barbie had also made similar allegations when she discovered she could not receive nor make phone calls.

"Like my wife's, my phone has been blocked and I am unable to receive or make regular calls. I know this is to stop me from communicating to our agents and coordinators. I encourage you comrades to be vigilant as I try to devise ways of reaching out to you," he tweeted.

Other candidates with less than 5% of votes

Other candidates had less than 5% of the total votes counted in an election where Opposition candidates said they would reject the outcome.

Electoral boss Byabakama in under pressure to deliver a free and fair poll with the Forum for Democratic Change (FDC) candidate Patrick Amuriat issued a warning to the electoral body.

“We warn Byabakama. He should know that he has an obligation to Ugandans not just Museveni,” Amuriat said.

Election rules in the country state that results must be declared 48 hours after voting closure.

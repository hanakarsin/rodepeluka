Kenyan Preacher Rev Evans Kariuki Leads Christians in Prayers for Joe Biden at US Supreme Court

  • Reverend Evans and his team led hundreds of Christians in praying for Joe Biden and his government at the US Supreme Court
  • The special prayer session saw the attendance of many Christians who were captured on camera praying for the country's number one
  • This came barely months after he held a unique Easter Service at a car parking lot

Being a leader, in politics for that matter, has never been a walk in the park and this quite a number of politicians can tell you for free.

One needs support from other leaders and citizens but above all, God's guidance, wisdom and protection.

Prayers for Joe Biden

It is for this particular reason that US-based Kenyan Reverend Evans Kariuki thought it wise to pray for the country's number one, Joe Biden and the entire US government.

Reverend Evans Kariuki who is the head of Firebrand Nation on Sunday, September 12, led a throng of his congregants and other Christians from different parts of America for special prayers for Biden.

The special prayer session was held just outside America's Supreme Court and saw the attendance of thousands of Christians.

Sharing about the prayer event, the Reverend through his Instagram page shared videos and photos from the session followed by captions derived from the Bible.

"Isaiah 9:7-Of the increase of his government and peace, there shall be no end, upon the throne of David, and upon his kingdom, to order it, and to establish it with judgment and with justice from henceforth even forever. The zeal of the LORD of hosts will perform this," Rev Evans captioned one of his posts.

Well, his deeds impressed many people across the world including his countrymen back in Kenya who lauded him for making them proud in the international arena.

Quite a number even pleaded with him to do the same in Kenya where politics has been becoming toxic as the 2022 General Election draws ne.

Not long ago, TUKO.co.ke reported Reverend Evans made headlines after hosting yet another huge Christian event in the US.

Reverend Evans Kariuki could not let the bans or the coronavirus scare deter him from holding one of the most important services in Christians' calendar, the Easter Service.

With churches closed, the Reverend, with the help of his team, held a Sunday service over the Easter Holiday at the City Wide Parking Lot.

The parking lot was filled to capacity with cars carrying congregants who followed the service dubbed City-Wide Parking Lot Easter Service at the comfort of their rides.

The team considered all the necessary measures set by governments to curb the spread of coronavirus by ensuring the cars were parked about a meter away from each other.

Judging by comments on posts he shared on Instagram, it was clear the service was a total success as people also came out in large numbers to worship with the Reverend despite the coronavirus scare.

Kariuki's Easter Service amid coronavirus scare came just days after he pleaded with the Kenyan government to allow churches to be opened so people could repent and also pray for healing from COVID-19.

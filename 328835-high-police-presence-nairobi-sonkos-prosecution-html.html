High police presence in Nairobi ahead of Sonko's prosecution

- Traffic was on Monday morning diverted to adjacent roads while hawkeyed police maintained guard around Milimani Court

- Mike Sonko arrived at the Milimani Anti Corruption Court handcuffed alongside another accused person

- He is set to face graft related counts following the alleged loss of KSh 357 million from county coffers

- Among those representing Sonko is Senator Kipchumba Murkomen (Elgeyo Marakwet), Mutula Kilonzo (Makueni)

Heavy security deployment has been witnessed in Nairobi on the morning of Monday, December 9, as Governor Mike Sonko awaits being arraigned.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt that high police presence has been witnessed around the Milimani Anti Corruption Court where Sonko, alongside his six co accused, are set to face graft charges.

Sonko was arrested on Friday, December 6, in Voi by anti graft sleuths following orders by DPP Noordin Haji who said investigations had revealed the governor had a case to answer concerning the loss of KSh 357 million.

Several leaders have, however, come out guns blazing describing Sonko's arrest and airlifting to Nairobi as uncalled for arguing that the governor would have just presented himself in court.

Section of Sonko's supports had threatened to take to the streets and protest what they termed as mishandling of the governor during his dramatic arrest on Friday.

The police warned that any authorised picketing will be met by the full force of the law.

Sonko also cautioned his supporters against staging a demonstration urging them to offer him moral support from home

This comes even as the Council of Governors (CoG) says it is seeking to have an emergency meeting with President Uhuru Kenyatta to talk about the fate of Nairobi county.

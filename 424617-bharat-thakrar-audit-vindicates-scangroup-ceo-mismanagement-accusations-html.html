Bharat Thakrar: Audit Vindicates Scangroup CEO from Mismanagement Accusations

  • Bharat Thakrar and chief finance officer Sabyabrata Das were suspended to pave way for investigation
  • WPP Scangroup PLC explained that Bharat had exited as both the CEO, board member and director of the company's affiliates and subsidiaries.
  • WPP Scangroup internal team and external investigators opened a probe to establish whether or not Thakrar committed an offence

WPP Scangroup has cleared its former chief executive officer Bharat Thakrar of gross misconduct.

The company's internal team and external investigators opened a probe to establish whether or not Thakrar committed an offence in his capacity as a senior executive.

In a statement on Tuesday, August 31, the board secretary Winnie Jumba said the investigation did not uncover any substantial against Thakrar and former chief finance officer Sabyabrata Das.

“The investigation did not identify items of a material nature that required adjustment to the results of the company or the group for the year ended December 31, 2020 or to the balance sheet at that date,” Jumbo stated.

Bharat and Das were suspended on February 19, to pave way for investigations.

"The board has delegated authority to an interim chief operating officer Alec Graham to ensure the continued management of the company with the support of the board and the senior executives of the company's subsidiaries," part of the statement released by Scangroup read.

Later on March 23, WPP Scangroup PLC explained that Bharat had exited as both the CEO, board member and director of the company's affiliates and subsidiaries.

The announcement further underpinned that the ongoing investigations against him and chief finance officer Satyabrata Das will continue, resignation notwithstanding.

Bharat made history as the first CEO of a firm listed at the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) to be suspended over ethical misconduct.

Bharat became Scangroup's CEO in 1999 and had an open-ended contract whose exit clause was marked as Not Application.

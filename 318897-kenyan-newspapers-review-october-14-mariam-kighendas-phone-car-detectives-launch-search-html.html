Kenyan newspapers review for October 14: Mariam Kighenda's phone missing as detectives launch probe

Newspapers on Monday, October 14 continued to give wide coverage to Kenya's stellar performance in athletics with Eliud Kipchoge and Brigid Kosgei being the centre of all attention.

The dailies also focused on the Kibra by-election campaigns where leaders allied to Jubilee Party asked the IEBC to remove Imran Okoth from the race claiming he was behind violence extended on its campaign caravan.

1. Daily Nation

Detectives investigating the Likoni ferry accident are looking for Mariam Kighenda's mobile phone which they say was not found in the car when it was recovered.

According to widower John Wambua, his wife Kighenda spoke to him on phone shortly before tragedy struck.

According to investigators, when the car was recovered it's ignition was off but the windshield wipers were found to be on.

Another angle detectives are pursuing is to establish whether or not the car's engine was running before it plunged into the ocean.

Inside the car, detectives found stickers with the religious inscriptions and some of them dated back to 2016.

2. The Star

Government Spokesman Cyrus Oguna has dispelled claims that Kenya Navy personnel interfered with the status of the vehicle that sunk at the Likoni Channel.

Oguna was responding to claims that Navy divers might have interfered with the gear lever of the saloon car so as to shift blame from Kenya Ferry Services (KFS) to the vehicle's occupants.

"There have been rumours that the navy went down and interfered with the gear for compensation purposes. How can that happen? The way the car was when it sank, was the same way it was retrieved. The only difference is that there were two lives when it went in and the lives were no more when it came out," said you Oguna.

According to the spokesperson, a team of detectives were investigating the accident and a report on findings will be made public soon.

3. People Daily

Even after the multi-agency team managed to pull out the wreckage containing two bodies from the Likoni Channel, it has emerged that teams involved had bitter rivalry between them.

The Kenya Navy has been on the receiving end for allegedly scheming a way that will see them receive all the credit and praise for the mission's success.

They have been accused of sidelining private divers who also played a key role in the mission. On Wednesday, October 9 they allegedly delayed pulling out the car just to ensure they got all the credit the following day.

Government Spokesman Cyrus Oguna has, however, dismissed the claims and termed them as unfounded.

"Kenya Navy was involved in the recovery mission in Zanzibar and Mwanza ferry accidents in Tanzania in 2011 and 2012. In 2000 the divers retrieved bodies from ill-fated KQ flight 43 in Abidjan Ivory coast where they braved the strong currents and retrieve the bodies 50 meters deep," said Oguna.

4. Taifa Leo

Relatives of people who died in the Ethiopian Airlines plane crash on March 10 are set to start receiving bodies of their kin who perished.

This comes after at least six months a period Which investigators said will help them identify the bodies through DNA analysis since most of them who found in pieces.

The family of John Karanja is among those who are set to pick bodies. Five of his relatives lost their lives in the accident.

Karanja lost his wife, daughter and three grandchildren in the accident. They are expected to lay them to rest on Thursday, October 17.

5. The Standard

The Anglican Church of Kenya has pressed upon the government to lower its appetite on loans in a move to cushion citizens from high taxes.

Ack Bishop Jackson Ole Sapit said that the high taxes had created an environment that was not welcoming to business investments and had made Kenyans become poorer.

"Excess taxation creates and unfavourable environment for investment which is important for economic growth. This means people will have little economic activity.My observation shows that greater percentage of these funds which the government borrows goes into pockets of corrupt instead of undertaking development projects,"said the man of God.

This, however, comes a week after Parliament raised the borrowing ceiling to KSh 9 trillion to KSh 7.5 trillion which had been proposed, meaning that the government has a bigger room to borrow more.

By June 2019 the public debt stood at about KSh 5.8 trillion and economic pundits claim it may shoot up to KSh 7 trillion by the time President Uhuru Kenyatta leaves power in 2022.

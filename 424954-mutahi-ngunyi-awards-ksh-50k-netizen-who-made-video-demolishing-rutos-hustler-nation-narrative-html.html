Mutahi Ngunyi Awards KSh 50k to Netizen Who Made Video Demolishing Ruto's Hustler Nation Narrative

  • An upcoming comedian and entertainer known by the name Official Aluda made a video that sought to deconstruct Ruto's hustler narrative
  • The viral video featured various clips including one which captured Ruto who is seen addressing a section of supporters
  • The trending video caught Ngunyi's attention prompting him to award Aluda KSh 50,000 for demolishing the hustler narrative

Political analyst Mutahi Ngunyi does not hide his strong dislike for Deputy President William Ruto and anything associated with the former Eldoret North KANU stalwart.

And that included jumping onto every available opportunity to lambast the Jubilee politician who has his eyes trained on the 2022 presidential contest.

Whether via his Twitter handle or Fourth Estate platform, Ngunyi must have his way.

Recently, an upcoming comedian and entertainer simply known by the name Official Aluda made a video that sought to deconstruct Ruto's hustler narrative.

The viral video featured various clips including one which captured Ruto who is seen addressing a section of supporters at his Karen residence as he champions the hustler politics.

He said contrary to the belief that he is rich and wealthy, he is leading a mass following of common mwananchi and those living from hand to mouth.

The trending video caught Ngunyi's attention prompting him to award Aluda KSh 50,000 for demolishing the hustler narrative.

Outlawed Mungiki

In January 2021, Ngunyi likened the hustler movement to the outlawed Mungiki sect which previously thrived in Central Kenya.

In a tweet on Tuesday, January 19, the current affairs commentator claimed the DP's hustler nation had only taken roots among the Gikuyu, Embu, Meru Association (GEMA).

"One, the spirit of the hustler nation is the spirit of Mungiki. Two, hustler nation is only in GEMA, nowhere else in Kenya. Three, there is no hustler nation in Kalenjin. Only kikuyus. This is identity theft," he argued.

Ngunyi who has been a fierce critic of Ruto since the famous March 2018 handshake between President Uhuru Kenyatta and former prime minister Raila Odinga, compared the second in command to a best man who had invaded the groom's bedroom saying he was busy "romancing" the president's things.

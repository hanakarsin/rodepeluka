Consignment of 410k AstraZeneca COVID-19 Vaccine Doses from UK Lands at JKIA

  • Health Chief Administrative Secretary Mercy Mwangangi said the donation would go a long way in combating the virus

Kenya's capacity to vaccinate its citizens against coronavirus remains robust after the government received an additional 410,000 doses of AstraZeneca vaccine doses.

A plane carrying a consignment of the COVID-19 vaccine doses landed at the Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) from the United Kingdom (UK) on Saturday, July 31.

Speaking at the airport, Health Chief Administrative Secretary Mercy Mwangangi said the donation would go a long way towards bolstering Kenya’s journey towards realising herd immunity.

“Our capacity for vaccine deployment remains robust. As is common knowledge our efforts have been greatly affected by supply constraints. When we receive donations like these from friends and well-wishers, it’s a deserved shot in the arm for our vaccination efforts," she noted.

UK vaccine pledge

The consignment was the first batch of 817,000 COVID-19 vaccine doses the UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson pledged to send to Kenya during President Uhuru Kenyatta's three-day visit.

In a joint statement released on Wednesday, July 28, by the two governments, the UK said the Oxford-AstraZeneca doses would be shipped to Kenya soon.

Half of the doses were to be shipped through a direct bilateral donation and the remaining half through a UK donation to the COVAX facility.

Kenya also expects to receive 271,440 doses of Pfizer from the COVAX facility and another 1,760,000 doses of Pfizer from the US government.

The government had procured 13 million doses of Johnson and Johnson through the AVAT-AU mechanism whose delivery will start in August.

COVID-19 update

At the same time, the Ministry of Health announced 1,259 people tested positive for the coronavirus from a sample size of 8,081.

The positivity rate is now at 15.6%.

Total confirmed positive cases jumped to 203,213 and cumulative tests so far conducted also increased to 2,132,355.

At least five patients succumbed to the disease, one having occurred in the last 24 hours while four were late deaths reported after conducting facility record audits.

The new deaths brought the cumulative fatalities to 3,931.

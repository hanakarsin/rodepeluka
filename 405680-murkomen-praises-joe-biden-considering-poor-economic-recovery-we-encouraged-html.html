Murkomen praises Joe Biden for considering the poor in economic recovery: &quot;We are encouraged&quot;

Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen has praised the United States of America President Joe Biden for considering the poor in his economic recovery strategy.

While making a reference to a New York Times article titled, To Juice the economy, Biden bets on the poor, the lawmaker claimed 'hustlernomics' is real and is being implemented globally led by the US.

"Some people are telling us hustlernomics is a threat to national unity. Thank you @JoeBiden for showing the way. We are encouraged by global partners like you," Murkomen tweeted.

The sentiments were echoed by Kikuyu Member of Parliament Kimani Ichung'wah who is a member of the hustler nation being championed by Deputy President William Ruto.

"The hustler nation's bottom-up approach is all over. By empowering the base of our pyramid we shall also be expanding the tax base bringing more people into the taxpayers' bracket and growing our economy. People like @FredMatiangi can’t get it. May God open their eyes," the Tanga Tanga legislator said.

Biden's economic relief package

Biden has unveiled a $1.9 trillion (KSh 190 trillion) economic relief package targeting the lowest-paid workers in the US and those who are unable to work at all.

In this, the Democrat who took the oath of office in January plans to uplift the lives of low-income Americans through the provision of $ 1400 (KSh 153,000) cheques to individuals in what is dubbed as the American rescue plan.

While Ruto's allies have praised the approach, several Kenyan leaders have openly called on the DP to drop the potentially dangerous campaign strategy that they have noted could drag the country into murky waters.

Hustler narrative raises concerns

The latest was Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i who termed it a threat to national security.

Speaking at a developmental tour in Murang'a on Thursday, March 11, the tough-talking CS said the hustler narrative could result in class war in the country.

“Those are the kind of things that are giving us serious problems in matters of cohesion and general security in the way we conduct our politics,” Matiang'i said.

Ruto and his Tanga Tanga brigade have, however, maintained it was time to change the political conversation in Kenya by moving from ethnicity to the real issues affecting ordinary Kenyans at the grassroot level.

The former Eldoret North MP has on several occasions argued he won't stop empowering his fellow "hustlers" as champions his 2022 presidential bid.

“Yes. It is time for change, time to talk and walk the hustler narrative. Not about sharing power but empowerment. Not about positions but jobs for our people, not about leaders but ordinary citizens. This wheelbarrow conversation will continue,” he said.

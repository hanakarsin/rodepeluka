Mike Sonkos management by threats and intimidation growing by the day

- The Nairobi Governor is synonymous with throwing fits of rage especially while cornered

- He stormed Kangemi Health Centre and harassed medics blaming them of laxity

- Sonko belittled his executives in front of the public accusing them of colluding with cartels to steal government drugs

- The county chief verbally suspended Nairobi county health executive Hitam Majevda over alleged incompetence

- The governor even blackmailed Health Cabinet Secretary Sicily Kariuki saying he could talk to the president directly

Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko is undoubtedly a combative leader who in many instances has thrown caution and decorum to the wind, with his recent actions proving he could get things messier.

Sonko suspended County Health Executive Hitam Majevda and threatened Thomas Ogaro, the Director of Medical Services in Nairobi, accusing the two of being part of cartels who have wrecked the city's health sector.

"My county health minister Majevda is a tourist fond of hopping from one plane to another touring America, Australia and other countries while our people are dying for lack of drugs in hospitals. I have suspended you from now. You have 14 days to show cause why you should not be sacked," Sonko said on Wednesday, August 1 to a shocked Majevda who was sacked in full glare of the public.

He further cast aspersions on Ogaro who had served under his predecessor Evans Kidero claiming he was among individuals working with Kenya Medical Supplies Agency (KEMSA) to divert drugs to private chemists.

Sonko ordered the official too to stand before a wildly cheering crowd as he lectured him.

As if the incidents were not enough portrayal of arrogance, the governor unsparingly belittled Majevda who had accompanied him during a random visit to Kangemi Health centre which had a shortage of drugs.

"You may be learned as a doctor but I don't care because I am the governor of Nairobi. The people who voted for me must be taken care of. You are blackmailing me through poor service delivery together with your cartels," he accused the CEC.

The Health Executive had a difficult time explaining there was a shortage of drugs because of payment arrears of over KSh 200 million the county government owed KEMSA, making the latter to cut supplies.

"I don't want foolish and mediocre people in my government. We paid KEMSA KSh 52 million and they promised to supply drugs. Even you doctors are operating chemists here to fleece patients. I will sack and close those pharmacies until service delivery improves," Sonko retorted.

Health Cabinet Secretary Sicily Kariuki was not spared by the governo's arrogance as the Sonko without courtesy, asked her to prevail on KEMSA to resume supplies during the breastfeeding week launch event at Pumwani Maternity Hospital on Thursday, August 2.

"Even you CS whether you listen or not, we need KEMSA to ensure our hospitals have drugs because the debts they are claiming were incurred by Kidero. If you cannot help, I can talk to the president directly. Shame is not part of me," the governor retorted.

Sonko also stormed maternity sections in Kangemi and Pumwani without caring about the privacy of the mothers in a bid to inquire if they had paid for the services or not and even gave some of them cash to buy bleach as directed by nurses.

The governor's theatrics prompted Kariuki to prevail on KEMSA to begin supplying drugs to county hospitals from Friday, August 3.

"After quick consultation with my team and as per your request, KEMSA will resume supply of drugs to your county tomorrow. I appreciate there were pending bills and you’ll continue carrying burden but this should not stop public from being served," Kariuki told Sonko during her address.

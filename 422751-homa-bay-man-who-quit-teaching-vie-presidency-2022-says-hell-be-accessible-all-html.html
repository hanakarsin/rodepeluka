Homa Bay Man Who Quit Teaching to Vie for Presidency in 2022 Says Hell be Accessible to All

  • Felix Jarongo Odeny said he is determined to ascend to the top seat in the coming 2022 General Election
  • Odeny stated he is using Facebook as his main campaign platform where he is selling manifestos and reaching out to his voters
  • He described himself as a humble leader who is for the citizens of Kenya and has experienced the pains and disappointments of the voters over the years

A teacher in Homa Bay county has quit his teaching job to vie for the presidency in the coming 2022 General Election.

Felix left teaching for politics.

Felix Jarongo Odeny, 44, of Kanyadhiang’ within Kendu Bay, said he will vie for the presidency after quitting his teaching job, having graduated with a Master’s Degree in Linguistics from Maseno University.

Odeny stated that he is determined to ascend to the top seat come 2022, adding that he will be using Facebook as his main campaign platform where he is selling manifestos and reaching out to voters.

He moved to Tanzania in the year 2011 to pursue his dream of being a lecturer of languages at the St. Augustine University of Tanzania in Mwanza after teaching at Nyahere Girls for a few years.

“I had wanted to register for my PhD when I got back to the country but had to suspend it due to financial constraints. I did not want to go through the same financial challenges I went through while pursuing my master’s degree,” said the aspiring president.

Odeny stated his inspiration had come from the communal lifestyle of the Tanzanian people and the simplicity with which they lead their lives.

He quit his lecturing job in Tanzania in 2018 and moved back to Kenya to strategise how he will win the top seat.

He explained that his main job currently is strategising on how to ascend the seat as an independent candidate.

“I chose the presidency because change needs to begin from the top of the chain so that every other seat will follow in line. I am not a proud person to dismiss the other seats, I started lower than that by being a teacher, however I believe that the time is ripe for people like me to come out and change the leadership of this country,” said Odeny.

He described himself as a humble leader who is for the citizens of Kenya and has experienced the pains and disappointments of the voters for several years.

“Over 50 years after independence and our people are still fighting diseases such as cholera, bilharzia and even unemployment which should not be the case for a country as ours. We therefore need to change our perception of leadership if we are to experience a brighter future,” he added.

When asked about his family, Odeny was quick to respond that in as much as he is a family man, putting his family in the limelight is a risk he is yet to come to terms with, adding that he has a female running mate in mind that would be revealed when theright time comes.

He currently has no financial sponsors but relies solely on his will and ability to change the mindset of the people of Kenya on new leadership and support from family members.

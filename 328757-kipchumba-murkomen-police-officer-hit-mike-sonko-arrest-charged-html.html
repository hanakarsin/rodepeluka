Kipchumba Murkomen wants police officer who tried to hit Mike Sonko during arrest charged

- On Friday, December 6, Governor Mike Sonko was arrested over graft allegations

- The county boss protested the arrest which led to a scuffle with police officers

- National Police Service (NPS) said he will face assault charges among others

- A video shared online showed one of the officers tried to hit handcuffed Sonko

- Senator Kipchumba Murkomen now wants the police officer also charged

Elgeyo Marakwet senator, Kipchumba Murkomen, has appealed to Inspector General of Police (IGP) Hillary Mutyambai to have police officer who tried to hit Governor Mike Sonko during dramatic arrest charged.

In a video shared by Murkomen on his Twitter page, a police officer in blue uniform is seen charging at the gold-loving county boss after he told him to keep off.

The vocal Rift Valley politician was responding to earlier report that the National Police Service (NPS) was intending to file assault charges against the county boss.

"In the process of his arrest, he assaulted and injured the senior police officer leading the team. Consequent to these acts of lawlessness against lawful police action, the police will prefer assault and other related charges against the governor," read a statement by Police Spokesperson Charles Owino.

Owino condemned Sonko's unruly behavior, violence and abuses he allegedly hurled at one arresting officer when he refused to board a plane from Voi to Nairobi during the arrest.

The flamboyant yet controversial city politician was cornered in Voi where a scuffle ensued between him and police before he was finally handcuffed.

He was airlifted to Nairobi where he was taken into Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) headquarters in Upperhill before he was taken to court.

Currently he is under police custody in undisclosed location after the court denied him bail.

Sonko was arrested on Friday, December 6, over irregular procurement and payment of over KSh 357 million to business entities that were given contracts by the county.

His arrested has since drawn a lot of criticism from various factions with some claiming the city boss best known for his impromptu raps was a victim of political witch hunt.

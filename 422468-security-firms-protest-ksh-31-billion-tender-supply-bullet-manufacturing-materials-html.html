Security Firms Protest KSh 3.1 Billion Tender to Supply Bullet Manufacturing Materials

  • The tender is for the supply of, among other things, assorted powder, primers, brass strips, bullets, copper-clad steel and carbide tools
  • Kenya Ordnance Factories Corporation (KOFC) in Eldoret, Uasin Gishu county, manufactures small arms ammunition
  • President Uhuru Kenyatta recently commissioned a small-arms factory at the National Security Industries in Ruiru

Local security firms are up in arms after the Kenya Ordnance Factories Corporation (KOFC) awarded a Chinese company a KSh 3.1 billion tender to supply bullet manufacturing materials.

Some of the potential suppliers claimed to the media that they would have offered better and lower prices than the firm that got the tender.

Beijing-based firm awarded the tender

The Beijing-based firm has already been given a notification to enter into a contract to supply the materials.

A letter dated June 2, 2021, to the firm stated: “Please confirm your acceptance within 14 days with effect from date of this letter and be prepared for signing of the contract.”

The tender is for the supply of, among other things, assorted powder, primers, brass strips, bullets, copper-clad steel and carbide tools.

It is unknown whether the Chinese firm has signed the contract even as other interested companies complain of being overlooked.

Officials at DoD refused to comment on the matter

Officials at the Department of Defence headquarters refused to comment on the matter, terming it a security issue.

One of the materials suppliers said he knows of a company that had offered a much lower price and would have supplied better services but was locked out.

“We suspect something happened, and we seek fairness for this. However, the prices of the company awarded are very high compared to other firms that offered to supply the same quality material at fairer prices,” said the official.

KOFC is a state corporation under the Ministry of State for Defence mandated to manufacture hardware, machinery and equipment.

It is Kenya’s only ammunition production firm and has two divisions: ammunition production and general engineering.

Its main customers include all Kenyan security agencies, approved licensed arms dealers, approved governments and international bodies.

The corporation, which is based in Eldoret, Uasin Gishu county, manufactures small arms ammunition.

Small-arms factory in Ruiru

President Uhuru Kenyatta recently commissioned a small-arms factory at the National Security Industries in Ruiru.

The head of state described it as the beginning of the country’s plans to become a significant weapons manufacturer.

The factory was built at KSh 4 billion and can produce 12,000 assault rifles a year, with 60% locally manufactured components.

“This ground-breaking initiative will allow us to lay the foundation for addressing the high cost of weapons acquisition, free us from the complex foreign export approval processes currently in place,'' Uhuru said.

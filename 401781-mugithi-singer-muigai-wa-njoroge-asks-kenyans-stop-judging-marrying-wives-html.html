Gospel singer Muigai Wa Njoroge asks Kenyans to stop judging him for marrying two wives

- Muigai Wa Njoroge was speaking during the funeral ceremony of the late Mugithi musician Mighty Salim where he asked fans to let celebrities live their lives

- His fans commended him for taking care of his two wives and bringing them to the limelight

- Though he claims to be born again and even sings gospel songs, his family life is not in tandem with Christianity which frown at polygamy

While polygamy is allowed in Kenya, there are still people who due to religious stands or financial status, still condemn those who engage in the practice, and controversial Gikuyu gospel artist Muigai Wa Njoroge is asking Kenyans to stop judging him.

The singer was speaking during the funeral service of the late Mighty Salim where he explained that he had no problem having two wives.

''Stop judging me for having two wives. Me having a second wife is not a sin, we have our lives as musicians. Some people who are here today have more than one wife but nobody asks them since they are not famous. But when God helps Muigai to add one more wife, the whole world starts fighting me," he said.

The Kigutha singer advised Kenyans to stop judging celebrities for making personal decisions.

“You want me to grow old so I can get another one? I am not a nun. Let us live our lives. I loved her and I could not live her to stray around with my kids.” added Wa Njoroge.

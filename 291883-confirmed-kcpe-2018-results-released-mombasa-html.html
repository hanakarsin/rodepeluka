Confirmed: KCPE 2018 results to be released in Mombasa

- KNEC said results would be released by Education CS Amina Mohammed at Star of the Sea Primary School

- The announcement came a day after Uhuru hinted Kenyans would know the outcomes on Monday, November 19

- A total of 1,060,759 pupils sat the 2018 Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) exams

- The president said every pupil would get a chance at secondary school regardless of their scores

The 2018 Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) exam results are set to be released on Monday, November 19, TUKO.co.ke has learnt.

The crucial results are expected to be released at the Star of the Sea Primary School in Mombasa County at around 1pm.

Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) said the results would be announced by Education Cabinet Secretary Amina Mohammed.

The examination body also provided information on how students and parents can receive the results instantly via mobile phones ahead of the release.

President Uhuru Kenyatta on Sunday caused a brief moment of panic after he inadvertently said the KCPE results would be announced on Monday.

Speaking during the launch of Gachororo PCEA Church in Juja, the president found himself saying, “and when the results are announced tomorrow ( which is today)," much to the surprise and amusement of everyone present.

He quickly rectified his mistake while acknowledging his slight error adding, “ah, now what have I said?”

And it now turns out he was actually right.

Despite the amusing error in his speech, the president was also quick to offer a word of encouragement to the pupils who were eagerly awaiting to know their fate as far as the results were concerned.

“When we will announce the results, I want to tell our children that because we know you did your best, do not be afraid just because you attained a particular grade because, like I said, the most important thing is that everyone who sat the Standard Eight exam will be admitted to Form One,” he said.

The release came barely 20 days since completion of the last paper which is a shorter duration of waiting compared to 2017.

A total of 1,060,759 pupils sat the 2018 Kenya Certificate of Primary Education exams.
